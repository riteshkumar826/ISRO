Explore ISRO - Indian Space Research Organisation

Discover the Space Missions, Innovations, and Achievements of ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of the Government of India, headquartered in Bengaluru, India. It was founded in 1969, and since then, it has made groundbreaking achievements in space exploration, satellite technology, and space research. ISRO is known for its cost-effective and ambitious missions, making space accessible to all.

ISRO has launched a variety of significant missions, both within India and internationally. Some of the prominent missions include:

  • Chandrayaan-1: India's first lunar probe that helped discover water on the Moon.
  • Chandrayaan-2: A follow-up mission to explore the lunar surface further.
  • Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission): India's first interplanetary mission to Mars, successfully reaching orbit in 2014.
  • PSLV-C37: The record-breaking mission that launched 104 satellites in one go.
